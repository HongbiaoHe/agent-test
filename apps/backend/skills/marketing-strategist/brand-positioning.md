# Brand Positioning

This skill defines where the brand sits in the customer's mind. It's the most upstream strategic skill — every downstream creative decision (Big Idea, Campaign Plan, visual aesthetic, voice register) flows from positioning.

This is NOT for campaign-level positioning (which Campaign Plan handles per-campaign). This is brand-level positioning: the long-term statement of who the brand is, who it's for, and why it matters.

In 2026, AI-generated marketing increasingly produces interchangeable output because brands skip positioning. Brands that nail positioning produce campaigns that compound; brands that don't produce one-off content that decays.

## When to Use

Fire when:
- Brief is at brand-strategy level ("help me position" / "品牌定位" / "who are we")
- Brand is 0→1 (new product / new brand with no defined positioning)
- Brand is repositioning for new market / category
- The Brief Tear Down output has [missing] Tone field AND no Brand Soul Enforcer profile exists
- User asks for marketing plan / strategy at brand level (not single campaign)

Skip when: existing brand with established positioning (route to Brand Soul Enforcer instead); single-campaign work without brand redefinition.

## Apply

### 1. The 4Cs Analysis (Company / Category / Competitors / Customers)

Before writing any positioning, map the four corners:

Company — What's authentic to this brand?
- Origin / founder story / heritage
- Capabilities / unique IP / process
- Values / culture / what they refuse to do
- What can they OWN that competitors can't?

Category — What category are we in (or creating)?
- Established category (joining) → must differentiate within
- Category creation (new) → must teach the category before positioning within it
- Category re-frame (existing words, new context) → most powerful when authentic

Competitors — Where is the white space?
- Map direct competitors on 2 axes (e.g., price vs polish; or speed vs quality)
- Find the unclaimed quadrant
- If no white space exists in current axes, find new axes
- Watch for "category convention" — things every competitor does that audiences are tired of

Customers — What job does the customer hire the brand to do?
- JTBD framing: customers don't want the product, they want the OUTCOME (Clayton Christensen — "people don't want a quarter-inch drill bit, they want a quarter-inch hole")
- Functional job (what they need done)
- Emotional job (how they want to feel)
- Social job (how they want to be perceived)

### 2. Status Level Declaration (1-5)

Declare the brand tier early — this constrains every downstream choice:

| Level | Tier | Visual marker | Verbal register |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Mass market / Discount | Bold colors, busy comp, price-led | Direct, urgent, "deal" language |
| 2 | Mid-mass | Lifestyle, accessible, friendly | Conversational, warm, helpful |
| 3 | Premium | Aspirational, considered, restrained | Confident, specific, considered |
| 4 | Luxury | Editorial, sparse, art-directed | Measured, authoritative, understated |
| 5 | Ultra-Luxury | Heritage, collectible, almost-no-marketing | Minimal, allusive, codes-not-claims |

Logo-blind test: if the logo were removed, the asset should still read at the correct tier.

### 3. Positioning Statement (the deliverable)

Produce a single positioning statement using JTBD template:

> For [target audience — specific demographic + psychographic]
> who need [job-to-be-done — outcome they're hiring the brand to deliver]
> we are a [category — either established or newly defined]
> that provides [differentiated benefit — not the feature, the outcome]
> Unlike [key competitor or alternative]
> we [key proof / reason to believe — what makes us the right hire for this job]

Example:
> For HK women aged 28-40 who need their skin to look better at the end of their workday than at the start (without a 12-step routine), L'Occitane is a heritage Provence-French skincare brand that delivers visible skin recovery through plant-based active formulations. Unlike clinical-feeling drugstore brands or aspirational-only luxury brands, we combine farmer-direct ingredient sourcing with clinically-tested formulas — so the skincare feels both rooted and effective.

### 4. The 4Cs Quality Test (Clarity / Consistency / Credibility / Competitiveness)

Before finalizing, score the positioning on:

- Clarity: Can a stranger understand this without context? If they need 3 paragraphs of explanation, rewrite.
- Consistency: Does every downstream creative decision (visual / verbal / motion) flow from this statement? If a Big Idea needs to contradict the positioning, the positioning is broken.
- Credibility: Is the "reason to believe" actually true? If the brand can't back it up, it's marketing fiction — will collapse at customer-experience layer.
- Competitiveness: Could a direct competitor say the EXACT same statement? If yes, it's not positioning, it's category language. Rewrite the differentiation.

Scoring:
- 4/4 = ready to anchor brand work
- 3/4 = ship if Clarity is solid; weak Competitiveness can be sharpened in Big Idea phase
- 2/4 = rewrite

## Defaults

- No JTBD declared by user → infer from product + audience; surface as [inferred] for confirmation
- No competitor named → surface "category default" assumption ("generic [category] brands") and ask for specific competitor if differentiation is unclear
- No status level declared → infer from price point + audience + product category (mass-market price + lifestyle aesthetic → Level 2; high price + aspirational → Level 3-4); surface for confirmation
- 0→1 brand with no truths to work from → surface this gap; positioning cannot be invented from nothing

## Hard Limits

- Never produce positioning that the brand can't deliver — positioning fiction collapses at customer-experience
- Never write positioning that competitors could equally claim — if Aesop + L'Occitane could both say it, it's not positioning
- Never skip the Status Level declaration — this is the single most important downstream constraint; without it, the brand fragments
- Never substitute virtue words ("quality / passion / innovation") for actual positioning content — these are placeholders, not positions
- Brand-level only — do NOT run this skill for campaign-level positioning; that's Campaign Plan's job

## Cross-Reference

- Brief Tear Down: provides product / audience / category context that feeds 4Cs analysis
- Market Discovery: if Competitors corner is empty / unknown, run Market Discovery first to map landscape
- Brand Soul Enforcer: receives the Status Level + verbal/visual implications from this skill; enforces them across production
- Big Idea / Concept Pitching: anchored by the positioning; the Creative Platform of a chosen Big Idea must express the positioning, not contradict it
- Campaign Plan: every campaign should ladder up to brand positioning; campaigns that don't, fragment the brand
